Happy Valley Roller Coaster - Inverse Proportional Function

Transformations：
\( \)
\( \)- When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x} \) to \( f(x) = \frac{-35}{x-7} - 10 \{ -5 < x < 5.3 \} \) , it is first vertically stretched by a factor of 35 and reflected over the x-axis, then horizontally translated 7 units to the right, and finally vertically translated 10 units downward with a domain restriction.
Domain and Range ：
\( \)-For the function \( f(x) = \frac{-35}{x-7} - 10 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, -5 < x < 5.3) \) , which is the interval specified in the function's definition; its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, -7.08 < y < 10.59) \) , determined by the domain restricting the range of the reciprocal term.

The inverse proportional function effectively models the steep, looping drop of a roller coaster, with the vertical stretch and downward shift simulating rapid acceleration and elevation loss during the ride. This demonstrates how mathematical scaling mirrors real-world energy transitions, as potential energy converts into kinetic energy with decreasing height, while the limited domain ensures the model represents only the realistic section of the track.

Zootopia Amusement Building - Rational Function7

Transformations：
\(-\) When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x^2} \) to \( f(x) = \frac{3.4}{(x-1.8)^2} - 7.5 \{ -0.4 < x < 1.27 \} \) , it is first vertically stretched by a factor of 3.4, then horizontally translated 1.8 units to the right, and finally vertically translated 7.5 units downward with a domain restriction.
Domain and Range ：
\(-\)For the function \( f(x) = \frac{3.4}{(x-1.8)^2} - 7.5 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, -0.4 < x < 1.27) \) , which is the interval defined for the function; its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, -6.80 < y < 4.60) \) , derived from the positive definiteness and boundedness of the squared reciprocal term within the domain.

This rational function captures the building’s concave facade that narrows at the center and widens at the edges, where the denominator’s power controls the smooth curvature and the downward translation aligns with the base of the structure. By restricting the domain, the model highlights the realistic front view of the architecture, showing how rational equations can represent real-world symmetry and spatial depth.

Sunway Lagoon Slide - Cubic Polynomial Function

Transformations：
\(-\) When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = x^3 + x \) to \( f(x) = -0.4(x+1.6)^3 + 0.2(x+1.6) - 1 \) , it is first vertically stretched by a factor of 0.4 and reflected over the x-axis, then horizontally translated 1.6 units to the left, and finally vertically translated 1 unit downward.
Domain and Range ：
\(-\) For the function \( f(x) = -0.4(x+1.6)^3 + 0.2(x+1.6) - 1 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, -\infty < x < \infty) \) , as cubic functions (odd-degree polynomials) have no inherent domain constraints; its range is also \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, -\infty < y < \infty) \) , since cubic functions are unbounded, and this property remains unchanged after scaling and translation.

The cubic function models the gradual start, steep middle drop, and smooth landing of a water slide. Its negative leading coefficient sets the direction of descent, and the vertical scaling produces a realistic slope transition. This design shows how polynomial functions can simulate motion changes—from slow acceleration to rapid descent and a gentle stop—reflecting both mathematical precision and physical realism.

Russian-style Amusement Park Building - Tangent Function

Transformations：
\(-\) When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = \tan x \) to \( f(x) = 2.3 \tan(0.4x - 6.8) + 5.7 \left\{ -\frac{1}{2}\pi < x < \frac{1}{2}\pi \right\} \) , it is first horizontally stretched by a factor of 2.5, then horizontally translated 17 units to the right, next vertically stretched by a factor of 2.3, and finally vertically translated 5.7 units upward with a domain restriction.
Domain and Range ：
\(-\)For the function \( f(x) = 2.3\tan(0.4x - 6.8) + 5.7 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, -\frac{1}{2}\pi < x < \frac{1}{2}\pi) \) , which is the given interval (with none of the tangent function’s vertical asymptotes falling within this interval); its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, -\infty < y < \infty) \) , as the tangent function can cover all real numbers, and this characteristic is not altered by scaling and translation.

The tangent function models the tall, pointed domes of Russian architecture through its periodic rise and steep vertical behavior. Adjusting amplitude and phase shift creates rhythmic repetition resembling real towers, demonstrating how trigonometric periodicity can capture architectural symmetry while domain restrictions convert infinite mathematical behavior into physical scale.

Mario Amusement Park - Building Sine Function

Transformations：
\(-\) When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = \sin x \) to \( f(x) = -0.8 \cdot \sin(0.8x + 0.2) + 2.9 \{ -5 < x \} \) , it is first horizontally stretched by a factor of 1.25, then horizontally translated 0.25 units to the left, next vertically stretched by a factor of 0.8 and reflected over the x-axis, and finally vertically translated 2.9 units upward with a domain restriction.
Domain and Range ：
\(-\)For the function \( f(x) = -0.8\sin(0.8x + 0.2) + 2.9 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, -5 < x < \infty) \) , limited by the given lower bound; its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, 2.10 \leq y \leq 3.70) \) , adjusted by vertical scaling and translation based on the sine function’s inherent range of \([-1,1]\).

The sine function models the smooth wave-like roof of the Mario Park building, where amplitude defines the height of each arch and the period controls the frequency of repetition. The reflection and scaling parameters balance symmetry and rhythm, illustrating how trigonometric models can express both aesthetic flow and structural harmony in architecture.

DisneySea Volcano Structure - Exponential Function

Transformations：
\(-\) When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = 2^x \) to \( g(x) = 10 \cdot 2^{0.3(x + 3.1)} + 0.8 \{ -12 < x \} \) , it is first horizontally stretched by a factor of \( \frac{10}{3} \) , then horizontally translated 3.1 units to the left, next vertically stretched by a factor of 10, and finally vertically translated 0.8 units upward with a domain restriction; when transformed to \( h(x) = 10 \cdot 2^{0.3(-x + 3.1)} + 0.8 \{ 2 < x \} \) , it is horizontally reflected over the y-axis based on the transformation of \( g(x) \) and then the domain is adjusted.
Domain and Range ：
\(-\)For the function \( g(x) = 10 \cdot 2^{0.3(x+3.1)} + 0.8 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, -12 < x < \infty) \) , determined by the given lower bound; its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, 2.35 < y < \infty) \) , since the exponential function \( 2^t > 0 \) and the exponential term has a lower bound within the domain. For the function \( h(x) = 10 \cdot 2^{0.3(-x+3.1)} + 0.8 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, 2 < x < \infty) \) , limited by the given lower bound; its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, 0.8 < y < 13.40) \) , as the exponential term approaches 0 as \( x \) increases and has an upper bound within the domain.

The exponential function represents the volcano’s steep growth from the base and gradual flattening near the peak. The horizontal reflection of one branch shows natural symmetry, and the scaling demonstrates how exponential growth can model nature-inspired forms—rapid at first and stabilizing toward the top—closely matching geological contours.

Roller Coaster - Logarithmic Function

Transformations：
\(-\) When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = \log_2 x \) to \( f(x) = -1.5 \cdot \log_2(x + 0.1) - 1.7 \{ 0 < x \} \) , it is first vertically stretched by a factor of 1.5 and reflected over the x-axis, then horizontally translated 0.1 units to the left, and finally vertically translated 1.7 units downward with a domain restriction.
Domain and Range ：
\(-\)For the function \( f(x) = -1.5\log_2(x + 0.1) - 1.7 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, 0 < x < \infty) \) —the argument of the logarithm \( x + 0.1 > 0 \) , and the domain is further restricted to \( x > 0 \) ; its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, -\infty < y < 3.30) \) , adjusted by scaling and translation, as the value of the logarithmic function can increase to infinity.

The logarithmic function effectively models a roller coaster’s steep initial climb that gradually flattens at higher points. Its reflection forms a descending path that represents controlled deceleration, showing how logarithmic relationships between height, time, and motion can depict realistic energy changes in physical systems.

Sunway Lagoon Pirate Ship - Quadratic Function

Transformations：
\(-\) When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = x^2 \) to \( f(x) = -0.14x^2 + 4.3 \{ -3 < x < 3.6 \} \) , it is first vertically stretched by a factor of 0.14 and reflected over the x-axis, then vertically translated 4.3 units upward with a domain restriction.
Domain and Range ：
\(-\)For the function \( f(x) = -0.14x^2 + 4.3 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, -3 < x < 3.6) \) , which is the interval defined for the function; its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, 2.49 < y < 4.30) \) , determined by the vertex (maximum value) of the downward-opening parabola and the smaller values at the endpoints of the domain.

The quadratic function demonstrates the symmetrical swinging motion of a pirate ship ride, where the vertex represents the highest point of potential energy. The coefficient controls the width of the curve, determining how fast the ride transitions between rising and falling, providing a mathematically accurate reflection of pendulum dynamics.

Shanghai Disneyland Main Entrance - Square Root Function

Transformations：
\(-\) When transforming the parent function \( f(x) = \sqrt{x} \) to \( f(x) = 4.5\sqrt{0.3(x + 16)} + 7.2 \{ -16 < x \} \) , it is first horizontally stretched by a factor of \( \frac{10}{3} \) , then horizontally translated 16 units to the left, next vertically stretched by a factor of 4.5, and finally vertically translated 7.2 units upward with a domain restriction.
Domain and Range ：
\(-\)For the function \( f(x) = 4.5\sqrt{0.3(x + 16)} + 7.2 \) , its domain is \( (x \in \mathbb{R}, -16 < x < \infty) \) —the radicand of the square root \( 0.3(x + 16) > 0 \) , requiring \( x > -16 \) ; its range is \( (y \in \mathbb{R}, 7.20 < y < \infty) \) , adjusted by scaling and translation, as the square root term is always positive.

The square root function captures the archway’s smooth rise that slows as it reaches higher points, balancing structural realism and aesthetic proportion. The horizontal translation and scaling create a natural height progression, illustrating how square root relationships can describe gradual spatial transitions in architectural design.
